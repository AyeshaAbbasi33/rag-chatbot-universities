NUST (National University of Sciences and Technology) - Admission Information 2026

ABOUT NUST
National University of Sciences and Technology (NUST) is one of Pakistan's premier public-sector universities, located in H-12, Islamabad. Established in 1991, NUST is globally ranked around 353 in the Times Higher Education World University Rankings and 67 in Asia. NUST is renowned for engineering, technology, computing, business, and natural sciences education. It attracts over 60,000 applicants annually for around 3,000 undergraduate seats.

UNDERGRADUATE PROGRAMS
NUST offers 40+ undergraduate programmes across these disciplines:
- Engineering & Technology
- Computing & Information Technology (including BS Computer Science, BS Data Science, BS Artificial Intelligence)
- Life Sciences & Medicine
- Arts & Humanities
- Natural Sciences
- Social Sciences
- Business & Management (Business Administration)

NUST also offers 60+ Masters programmes and PhD programmes across Engineering, IT and Computer Science, Business Studies, Social Sciences, Architecture, Natural Sciences, and Biotechnology.

ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA FOR UNDERGRADUATE PROGRAMS
- Minimum 60% marks required in FSc (Pre-Engineering, Pre-Medical, or ICS) or equivalent qualification.
- NUST does not accept NTS (National Testing Service) scores.
- Candidates with HSSC Computer Science group (Mathematics, Physics, Computer Science combination) are eligible to apply for all Engineering programs, with a condition to clear Chemistry as a remedial subject/course in the 1st semester after admission.
- FSc Pre-Medical students applying for Engineering programs without additional math must complete a condensed Mathematics course (8 weeks, online, self-paced) offered by NUST before admission, and must secure 60% marks in it.
- Candidates with HSSC Mathematics as a major subject are eligible to apply for Computing Programmes (BS Computer Science, BS Data Science, BS Bioinformatics, etc).
- O/A Level and other foreign equivalent qualifications require an equivalence certificate from IBCC (Inter Boards Coordination Commission), Pakistan, with minimum 60% marks in relevant subjects.
- DAE (Diploma of Associate Engineering) candidates can apply for admission in selected Engineering programmes on open merit.
- All candidates, including Matric/FA/FSc stream, must get their educational documents attested from IBCC before submitting to the university at the time of admission.

NUST ENTRY TEST (NET)
- NUST has its own entry test called NET (NUST Entry Test), required for most undergraduate programs.
- Candidates may also apply on the basis of ACT/SAT scores instead of NET. NUST's institutional code for ACT score reporting is 2915.
- NET-2026 (Series-4) registration was open until June 18, 2026.
- Foreign nationals (non-Pakistani) can also apply on the basis of NET, in addition to ACT/SAT.

MERIT GENERATION CRITERIA
- Merit is calculated using a weighted combination of academic qualification (FSc/A-Level/equivalent) and NET/ACT/SAT score.
- O/A Level candidates still in A-Level (final year) will be assigned 25% weightage to their O-Level equivalence marks per IBCC certification.
- Minimum 60% marks in SSC and HSSC (or equivalent) is mandatory regardless of test performance.
- NUST will not be responsible if HSSC or ACT/SAT results are not declared in time for merit calculation.

HOSTEL FACILITIES
NUST provides separate hostel facilities for male and female students, starting at approximately PKR 60,000 per semester with modern amenities.

MERIT LIST ANNOUNCEMENT
The merit list for Fall admissions is typically expected in August. Updates are posted at ugadmissions.nust.edu.pk/result.
